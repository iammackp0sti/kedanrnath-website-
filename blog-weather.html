Kedarnath Weather Month by Month

Seasonal Forecasts • June 2026 • 6 min read

Back to Blog Index

Weather in the Kedarnath Valley is highly volatile. An afternoon that starts bright and warm can transition to sub-zero rain or snowfall within minutes. To help you prepare for your pilgrimage, here is a month-by-month analysis of temperatures and safety conditions.

May: The Reopening

May marks the beginning of the yatra. The temple doors open around Akshaya Tritiya. Residual snow lies on paths. Days are cool (10°C to 15°C) and night temperatures linger near freezing (0°C to 5°C). Snow showers are common in early May.

June: The Peak Season

June offers the most stable and pleasant climate of the first half. Daytime temperatures rise to 18°C, while nights remain cold (5°C to 8°C). This is the busiest month for pilgrim congestion. Secure clean room bookings early.

July & August: The Monsoon Season

Heavy rainfall hits the valley in late July and August. Landslides and road blocks occur along the Rishikesh-Rudraprayag highway. Daytime temperatures hover around 12°C. While trekking is permitted, helicopter services are frequently suspended due to clouds and poor visibility. Travel with caution, carry heavy rain ponchos, and rely on hosts like Frontier House for local route status updates.

September: The Autumn Rebound

Monsoons recede in early September. The valley is lush green, skies clear up, and surrounding peak views are spectacular. Days are fresh (12°C to 15°C) and nights turn very cold (2°C to 4°C).

October & November: Peak Winter Transition

October brings sharp dry winds. Daytime temperatures stay below 10°C, and nights regularly plummet below freezing (-2°C to -5°C). The temple closes around Bhai Dooj (early November). Heavy thermal layers, woollens, and room heaters are mandatory.

Important Packing Rule

Regardless of which month you travel, always carry thermal body wear, a rain jacket/poncho, and warm gloves. High-altitude climate shifts do not follow calendar rules.

Stay Warm in Freezing Nights

Our family rooms at Frontier House are equipped with thick blankets, running hot water geysers, and room heaters to shelter you from mountain cold.
