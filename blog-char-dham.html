Char Dham Yatra Planning Guide: Unified Routes

Char Dham Staging • May 2026 • 8 min read

Back to Blog Index

The Char Dham Yatra is one of the most sacred pilgrimage circuits in Hinduism. It spans four holy shrines located in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand: Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. Here is our family guide on how to plan this journey.

1. The Clockwise Tradition

Following Hindu custom, the pilgrimage is traditionally undertaken in a clockwise (Pradakshina) direction, starting from west to east:

  1. Yamunotri: The source of the Yamuna River. Reached via Dehradun and Barkot.
  2. Gangotri: The source of the Ganges River. Reached via Uttarkashi.
  3. Kedarnath: The high-altitude shrine of Lord Shiva. Reached via Guptkashi and Gaurikund.
  4. Badrinath: The holy seat of Lord Vishnu. Reached via Joshimath.

2. Transport Regulations: Green Cards

If you are traveling by private car or tourist bus, your vehicle must obtain a registered "Green Card" from the transport department in Haridwar or Rishikesh. This certifies that the vehicle is physically fit for steep mountain roads and the driver is experienced in high-altitude driving.

3. Registration & Staging

Biometric registration is mandatory for all four shrines. You can register for all destinations simultaneously on the government portal. Maintain printed copies of your registration forms, as checkpoints at each base town verify registration status.

4. Finding Safe Rest Locations

A typical Char Dham Yatra spans 10 to 12 days. Finding clean, warm, and comfortable family stays at each destination is critical to prevent fatigue. Look for family-run guesthouses like Frontier House in Kedarnath that provide home-style hospitality, geysers, and room heaters.
