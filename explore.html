Sacred Shrine

The Spirit & Significance of Kedarnath

Kedarnath is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites in India, serving as a vital component of the prestigious Char Dham Yatra. Located in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand, the temple stands majestically at an altitude of 3,583 meters (11,755 feet) above sea level, framed by the breathtaking, snow-capped Himalayas and the gentle waters of the Mandakini River.

Dedicated to Lord Shiva, the Kedarnath Temple is believed to have been originally constructed by the Pandavas and later reconstructed by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century. The natural beauty and deep spiritual aura of the valley offer pilgrims a profound sense of devotion and immediate mental peace, making the challenging trek up the mountain a spiritually rewarding endeavor.

Travel Routes

How To Reach Kedarnath

We outline the general travel routes from major cities to Sonprayag and Gaurikund. (Please confirm active bus/taxi timetables locally on your departure dates).

1

From Gateway Cities

Most pilgrims start from Haridwar, Rishikesh, or Dehradun. You can take early morning buses or hire private taxis traveling to Guptkashi or Sonprayag (approx. 180-220 km road trip, 7-9 hours).

2

Sonprayag Hub

Sonprayag serves as the primary parking and transit zone. All private vehicles must park here. Shared local shuttle jeeps operate continuously to take you further to Gaurikund (5 km away).

3

Gaurikund Base

Gaurikund is the actual base point of the yatra. It features natural thermal springs and is the official starting line of the 16 km steep foot trek to Kedarnath Temple.

Kedarnath Trek Guide

The trek from Gaurikund is 16 km long and involves steep ascents.

  • Start your trek very early in the morning (4:00 AM) to reach the top before dark.
  • Pace yourself, walk slow, and stay hydrated with mineral water.
  • Carry light snacks and rainwear in a small backpack.

Helicopter Service Guide

Helicopter services operate from Phata, Sersi, and Guptkashi.

  • Bookings must be made exclusively online via government portals beforehand.
  • Frontier House is located just 500 meters from the Kedarnath helipad.
  • We are happy to guide you on local weather conditions affecting flight times.

Pony & Palki Assistance

For senior citizens, families, or children who cannot complete the 16 km climb on foot.

  • Ponies, horses, and palkis (palanquins) can be hired at Gaurikund base.
  • Rates are officially regulated and fixed by local municipal administration.
  • Please ask the Frontier House family if you need help finding authentic operators.

Seasonal Trends

Kedarnath Weather & Clothing Guide

The weather in the Himalayas shifts rapidly. Review our seasonal tips to ensure you package appropriate clothing.

Season Months Temperature Range Clothing Suggestions
Summer May – June 5°C to 18°C Light woollens, thermal innerwear, windcheaters, heavy blankets at night.
Monsoon July – August 8°C to 15°C Waterproof raincoats, umbrellas, waterproof boots, warm thermals. (Check road statuses).
Autumn September – October 0°C to 12°C Heavy woollen jackets, gloves, warm caps, thermal sets, double socks.
Winter Nov – April (Closed) Sub-zero Temple gates closed. Valley undergoes heavy snowfall. Stay is not active.
