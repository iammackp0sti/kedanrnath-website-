Best Time to Visit Kedarnath: Peak vs. Shoulder

Seasonal Planning • May 2026 • 6 min read

Back to Blog Index

Choosing the right month to embark on your Kedarnath pilgrimage is the single most important decision for a comfortable yatra. Let us break down the advantages and challenges of the peak spring and crisp autumn seasons.

1. The Spring Peak Window: May to June

This is the most popular time for yatris:

  • Pros: Moderate daytime temperatures (12°C to 18°C), beautiful flowering landscapes, and stable flight conditions for helicopters.
  • Cons: Heavy crowd volumes, long queues for Darshan (often 4 to 6 hours), and higher rates for local transport and lodging.

2. The Monsoon Shoulder Window: July to August

We advise caution during the rainy season:

  • Pros: Very low crowd volumes, immediate access for temple entry, and low rates.
  • Cons: High risk of cloudbursts, landslide blocks on mountain roads, and frequent suspensions of helicopter services.

3. The Autumn Gold Window: September to October

This is our family's recommended time for pilgrims seeking spiritual peace:

  • Pros: Crystal-clear blue skies, excellent views of the Chaukhamba and Kedarnath peaks, lower humidity, and manageable crowd volumes.
  • Cons: Freezing night temperatures (often dropping to -2°C in October). Heavy woollens and proper room heating are essential.

Our Recommendation

If you prefer comfortable temperatures, travel in late May or early June. If you seek peaceful darshans and clear mountain scenery, reserve your dates for late September or early October.
